I've spent the last few months testing every AI tool that promises to "do the work for you." Claude builds my features. ChatGPT generates my images. And Manus AI? That was the one I was most curious about — and the one that surprised me most, in both directions.

Manus launched in March 2025, hit a $125 million revenue run rate by December, and got acquired by Meta for an estimated $2 to $2.5 billion. Nine months from launch to a billion-dollar exit.

**But is the product actually good, or is it just good at raising money?**

Here's what I found after putting all three through real workflows.

## Manus Is Not a Chatbot — That Matters More Than You Think

Before comparing features, you need to understand what Manus actually is — because it's not what most people assume. Claude and ChatGPT are large language models with conversational interfaces. You type, they respond, you iterate.

Manus is an **AI agent**. You give it a task — "research the top 10 CRM tools and build me a comparison spreadsheet" — and it goes off and does it. It opens browser tabs, reads web pages, fills forms, writes files, and comes back with a finished deliverable. No babysitting required.

This is the core value proposition: hands-off execution. And when it works, it genuinely feels like the future.

<Callout type="info">
**Agent vs. chatbot is the key distinction.** Claude and ChatGPT answer questions and help you work. Manus tries to do the work for you. These are different products solving different problems — comparing them directly on the same criteria misses the point.
</Callout>

## How Manus Actually Works Under the Hood

Manus doesn't have its own frontier model. It's an orchestration layer that calls other models — Claude, Qwen, and others — selecting the best one for each step. Think of it as a project manager that delegates to specialized workers.

It spins up virtual machines, launches browsers, runs code, and coordinates multiple AI agents in parallel. When you ask it to research a topic, it might deploy five agents simultaneously to cover different sources, then synthesize the results.

The technical architecture is impressive. According to Manus's own reporting, the platform has processed over 147 trillion tokens and powered the creation of more than 80 million virtual computers since launch. Take the exact numbers with a grain of salt — they're self-reported — but the scale of infrastructure is real.

But architecture and execution are different things. The question is whether that infrastructure translates to reliable output.

## The Pros: Where Manus Delivers

### Background Execution

Unlike Claude or ChatGPT, which require you to stay in the conversation, Manus can handle long-running tasks entirely in the background. Kick off a research project, close your laptop, and come back to a completed report. This is genuinely useful for data collection, market research, and recurring monitoring tasks.

### Zero-Code Setup

You don't write scripts or configure workflows. You describe what you want in natural language, and Manus figures out the execution plan. Multiple G2 reviewers highlighted that they didn't need any technical knowledge to get agents running — a major advantage for freelancers, marketers, and solo operators.

### Browser Automation Built In

Manus can control a browser to perform web-based actions: filling forms, navigating sites, pulling data from pages that don't have APIs. It ships with a Chrome extension called Browser Operator that handles this directly.

### Wide Research Capability

For research tasks specifically, Manus shines. It deploys multiple agents simultaneously across different sources, which means it can compile comprehensive reports faster than you could manually. If your primary use case is "gather and organize information from the web," Manus is purpose-built for that.

## The Cons: Where Manus Falls Short

### The Credit System Is Punishing

Manus runs on credits, and they burn fast. Complex tasks can consume 500–900 credits each. The Standard plan gives you 4,000 credits per month for $20 — meaning you might get through 4 to 8 complex tasks before you're out.

Here's the real problem: **there's no way to preview credit cost before starting a task.** And if your credits deplete mid-task, Manus stops completely, leaving you with half-finished work and zero credits. The consumed credits are gone.

| Plan | Price | Monthly Credits | Complex Tasks (~600 credits each) |
|------|-------|----------------|----------------------------------|
| Standard | $20/mo | 4,000 | ~6-7 tasks |
| Customizable | $40/mo | 8,000 | ~13 tasks |
| Extended | $200/mo | 40,000 | ~66 tasks |

All plans include 300 daily refresh credits and 20 concurrent tasks. Credits don't roll over month to month.

<Callout type="warning">
**Watch your credits.** Multiple users report that Manus consumes credits even when tasks fail or require manual correction. Trial-and-error workflows get expensive fast. The 1,300 free trial credits sound generous until your first complex task eats 800 of them.
</Callout>

### Complex Tasks Break Down

Manus handles focused, single-tool workflows well. But as tasks get more complex — spanning multiple applications, requiring branching logic, or needing frequent adjustments — the reliability drops. Users on G2 report "needing to rerun agents often because outputs were incomplete or inaccurate."

The tool makes assumptions. It can skip steps or misinterpret instructions without telling you. You get a deliverable that looks complete but contains gaps or errors you only catch on review.

### Limited Integrations

For a tool that promises to automate your workflows, the integration list is surprisingly thin. It doesn't connect with many standard business tools, which means workflows that span multiple applications hit a wall quickly. If you're imagining a Zapier-level integration ecosystem, that's not what Manus is today.

### Performance Issues

Several reviewers report slowdowns, lag, and occasional freezes during demanding tasks. When you're running background agents that take 30+ minutes, a mid-task freeze that consumes credits without producing output is more than an inconvenience.

### The Black Box Problem

Manus is autonomous by design, but that autonomy comes with opacity. You can't easily see why it made certain decisions, what sources it prioritized, or how it structured its research. The deliverable lands in your inbox — fact-checking is on you.

## Claude: The Developer's Pick

Claude takes the opposite approach to Manus. Instead of doing the work for you, it makes you dramatically faster at doing the work yourself.

**Current models (March 2026):**

| Model | Context | API Cost (Input/Output per MTok) |
|-------|---------|----------------------------------|
| Opus 4.6 | 200K (1M beta) | $5 / $25 |
| Sonnet 4.6 | 200K (1M beta) | $3 / $15 |
| Haiku 4.5 | 200K | $1 / $5 |

**Where Claude wins:**

- **Coding.** Claude Opus 4.6 scores 80.9% on SWE-bench Verified, making it the best coding model available. According to The Pragmatic Engineer's 2026 developer survey, Claude Code is now the most-used AI coding tool among professional developers, surpassing even GitHub Copilot.
- **Long-form content.** Opus 4.6's 128K output token limit means it can generate roughly 96,000 words in a single call. For detailed reports, documentation, or blog posts, nothing else comes close on output capacity.
- **Accuracy and depth.** Claude consistently produces more thoughtful, nuanced output. It follows complex instructions more precisely and handles long documents better than any competitor.
- **Computer use.** Claude Sonnet 4.6 scores 72.5% on the OSWorld benchmark for computer use tasks — a massive jump from under 15% in late 2024, and strong enough to handle spreadsheets, forms, and desktop application workflows reliably.

**Pricing:** Free tier with limited Sonnet access. Pro at $20/month. Max at $100–$200/month for heavy users.

**The limitation:** Claude is more conversational than autonomous. It generally needs you in the loop — though Claude Code operates autonomously for development tasks, and the newer Claude Cowork tool (launched January 2026) brings a similar autonomous workflow to non-technical users. Still, it's not doing background research tasks the way Manus does.

## ChatGPT: The Swiss Army Knife

ChatGPT remains the most versatile AI assistant available. It might not be the best at any single thing, but it does everything reasonably well.

**Current models (March 2026):**

| Model | Context | Key Strengths |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| GPT-5.4 | 1M tokens | Reasoning, coding, computer use |
| GPT-5.2 Thinking | 200K | Deep reasoning mode |
| GPT-5.2 Instant | 128K | Fast responses, Go tier |

**Where ChatGPT wins:**

- **Ecosystem breadth.** Image generation (DALL-E 4), video creation (Sora 2), voice mode, file analysis, web browsing, plugins — ChatGPT does more things than any other single platform.
- **Memory.** ChatGPT remembers your preferences and context across sessions. This compounds over time — the more you use it, the more useful it gets.
- **Creative work.** For brainstorming, writing marketing copy, generating images, and creative ideation, ChatGPT consistently outperforms on versatility and tone control.
- **Price accessibility.** The new Go plan at $8/month gives you unlimited GPT-5.2 Instant access. No other major AI offers that much capability at that price point.

**Pricing:** Free tier. Go at $8/month. Plus at $20/month. Pro at $200/month.

**The limitation:** ChatGPT's breadth comes at the cost of depth. For specialized tasks like production coding, long-form technical writing, or autonomous task execution, purpose-built tools outperform it.

## The Meta Acquisition Changes Everything

Meta's acquisition of Manus — reported at $2 to $2.5 billion and announced in December 2025 — reshapes the conversation. The deal reportedly included a $500 million retention pool for employees, and China's Ministry of Commerce launched an investigation into export control compliance — highlighting the geopolitical dimension.

What this means for users:

**Potential upside:** Meta's infrastructure, data, and distribution could make Manus dramatically more capable. Integration with Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Meta AI could create the most widely distributed autonomous agent on the planet.

**Potential downside:** Meta's track record with acquisitions is mixed. Instagram thrived. Giphy got sold off. And Meta's incentives — advertising revenue, engagement metrics, data collection — may not align with a productivity tool's goals. If Manus becomes primarily a vehicle for Meta's ad-targeting AI, the product you signed up for might not be the product you end up with.

<Callout type="tip">
**If you're evaluating Manus today**, factor in the acquisition. The product's roadmap is now Meta's roadmap. That could mean better features and infrastructure — or it could mean pivoting toward Meta's platform priorities rather than your productivity needs.
</Callout>

## So Who Should Use What?

**Choose Manus if:**
- Your primary need is autonomous research and data collection
- You want tasks completed in the background without supervision
- You're non-technical and need AI to execute, not advise
- You're comfortable with the credit system and its unpredictability
- You're okay with Meta owning the platform and your data

**Choose Claude if:**
- You're a developer or write code professionally
- You need long-form, high-quality content generation
- You want the most accurate and thoughtful AI responses
- You use Claude Code for autonomous development workflows
- You care about AI safety and responsible development

**Choose ChatGPT if:**
- You want one subscription that covers everything (text, images, video, voice)
- You value the broadest ecosystem of features and integrations
- You're doing creative work, brainstorming, or marketing
- You want the cheapest entry point ($8/month Go plan)
- Memory and personalization matter to your workflow

## The Bottom Line

Manus AI is a genuinely innovative product that introduced a new paradigm — AI that executes instead of advises. The research capabilities are real, the background execution is valuable, and the zero-code approach makes it accessible.

But it's not a replacement for Claude or ChatGPT. It's a different tool for a different job. And it comes with real trade-offs: unpredictable credit consumption, reliability issues on complex tasks, limited integrations, and the uncertainty of Meta ownership.

The honest answer to "is Manus AI good?" is: **it's good at a narrow set of tasks and questionable outside that range.** If your work involves recurring research, data collection, or report generation, Manus can save you hours. If you need deep coding assistance, go with Claude. If you need a versatile AI Swiss Army knife, ChatGPT is still the best all-rounder.

The AI assistant market isn't a winner-take-all game. The right move in 2026 is picking the right tool for each job — and right now, that probably means using more than one.
